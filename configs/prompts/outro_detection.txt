MANY VIDEOS HAVE NO OUTRO AT ALL. Screen recordings, raw clips, tutorials,
gameplay, demos, and home videos usually just END with no ending sequence. Do
NOT invent an outro that is not there. Only report an outro if you can actually

An "outro" is a distinct ENDING SEQUENCE where the main content has stopped and
something clearly closing is shown, such as:
  - rolling end-credits (cast/crew names, editor/director text)
  - a montage or split-screen recap with credit text or a "SUBSCRIBE" call-to-action
  - a sponsor / brand end-card or logo sequence
  - a clear closing title card after the story concludes
It often (but NOT always) follows a fade-to-black or a hard scene cut. A frame is
NOT an outro just because it is near the end, is dark, or shows an application,
desktop, slide, or talking head — those are main content. In particular, a live
performance / stage / audience shot, an interview, or a person still talking is
MAIN CONTENT even if the lighting dims or it is close to the end. The outro is a
DISTINCT card/credits/logo sequence, not the tail of the performance itself.

It is acceptable to choose a frame slightly INTO the outro, but it is NOT
acceptable to choose a frame that still shows MAIN CONTENT. When in doubt, prefer

content, which is unacceptable. Only pick a frame if you are CONFIDENT it already
belongs to a genuine ending sequence.
